## The Challenge

### Three Simultaneous Integrity Issues (July 2023)
After 10 years of continuous production from this deepwater HPHT well, three critical issues developed:

1. **Casing Deformation** (9,200 ft MD)
   - Production casing ovalized from 8.681" ID to 7.8" ID
   - Unable to pass full toolstrings
   - Restricting all future intervention capability

2. **SSSV Failure** (2,800 ft MD)
   - Primary subsurface safety valve failed during routine test
   - Stuck in open position
   - Primary safety barrier compromised (regulatory issue)

3. **Sand Production Risk**
   - Unconsolidated zone beginning to produce trace sand
   - Risk of catastrophic sand production and equipment damage
   - Requires sand control installation

### Traditional Approach: Subsea Workover

## AI-Assisted Planning Process

### Problem: What Sequence?
**Question:** Which intervention should be performed first, second, third?

**AI Analysis Factors:**
1. **Dependency Mapping:** Which issues block others?
2. **Risk Assessment:** What failure modes exist?
3. **Cost Optimization:** Vessel selection and mobilization efficiency
4. **Technology Selection:** What tools can pass through restrictions?
5. **Regulatory Priority:** Safety barriers vs. production optimization

### AI Recommendation: Three Sequential Campaigns

**Campaign 1: Casing Patch (August 21, 2023)**
- **Why First?** Must restore tool access BEFORE other interventions
- **Logic:** SSSV insert valve and sand screen cannot pass through 7.8" restriction
- **Technology:** 40-ft expandable casing patch
- **Result:** ID restored to 8.45" - enables Campaigns 2 & 3 ‚úÖ

**Campaign 2: SSSV Insert Valve (September 5, 2023)**
- **Why Second?** Regulatory priority - restore primary safety barrier
- **Logic:** Quick wireline job (3 days) + critical barrier requirement
- **Technology:** Wireline retrievable insert valve inside failed SSSV
- **Result:** Primary barrier restored ‚úÖ

**Campaign 3: Sand Screen Installation (September 15, 2023)**
- **Why Third?** Requires full bore access + most complex operation
- **Logic:** Sand control is production optimization, not immediate safety risk
- **Technology:** Through-tubing premium sand screen
- **Result:** Zero sand production ‚úÖ

**Key Insight:** The AI determined that attempting any other sequence would either:
- Fail (tools can't pass restriction)
- Cost more (require workover rig)
- Take longer (serial dependencies)

## Execution Summary

### Campaign 1: Casing Patch (August 21, 2023)

**Vessel:** Light Well Intervention Vessel
**Duration:** 1 day
**Cost:** $600K

#### Execution
1. **ROV Intervention:** Open subsea tree (FMC HXT 15K)
2. **CT Deployment:** 2.375" coiled tubing RIH to 9,200 ft
3. **Patch Installation:**
   - 40-ft Enventure expandable patch
   - Unexpanded OD: 6.5" (passes through 7.8" restriction)
   - Expanded OD: 9.5" (conforms to 9.625" casing)
   - Expanded ID: 8.45"
4. **Pressure Test:** 10,000 psi for 30 minutes ‚úÖ
5. **Verification:** Caliper log confirms 8.45" ID ‚úÖ

**Result:** ‚úÖ Full tool access restored

### Campaign 2: SSSV Insert Valve (September 5, 2023)

**Vessel:** Light Well Intervention Vessel (re-mobilized)
**Duration:** 3 days
**Cost:** $550K

#### Execution
1. **ROV Intervention:** Open subsea tree
2. **Lockopen Failed SSSV:**

   - Wireline retrievable design (no workover for future replacement)
4. **Function Testing:**
   - Close valve from surface: ‚úÖ Success
   - Hold 5,000 psi differential: ‚úÖ Success (zero leakage)
   - Open valve from surface: ‚úÖ Success
5. **NSTA Verification:** Remote ROV inspection + pressure test data submitted

**Result:** ‚úÖ Primary safety barrier restored (regulatory requirement met)

### Campaign 3: Sand Screen Installation (September 15, 2023)

**Vessel:** Light Well Intervention Vessel (re-mobilized)
**Duration:** 5 days
