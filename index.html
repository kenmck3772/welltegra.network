How it works (Upload → Result)

Scope reminder: Alpha demo. Read-only, simulated processing. No live vendor integrations.

  1. Upload (user-initiated): Choose a file or paste text (CSV, PDF, XLS export, email). Client-side checks; nothing moves without your explicit action.
  2. Secure intake (per-client workspace): Stored inside your tenant. Access is limited to users you authorize; actions are logged.
  3. Normalize & parse (schema on read): Detect entities (dates, depths, units, activities, NPT phrases) and build structured JSON alongside the original.
  4. Units & terms standardization: Normalize depth/pressure/volumes to your standards; align synonyms (e.g., “POOH”, “pull out of hole”).
  5. Quality checks & provenance: Flag outliers, attach source line/page to every extracted field for auditability.
  6. Client-only “mistake-mining”: Patterns are suggested from your own history only, with supporting excerpts.
  7. Human-in-the-loop review: Supervisors adjust labels/units; edits enrich your private guidance library.
  8. Outcome views (static in alpha): Planner, Performer, Analyzer show the journey; all illustrative.
  9. Export: Clean JSON, PDFs, or CSV. Originals remain unaltered; derivatives carry full lineage.

Diagram is conceptual. Demo uses simulated data; no real uploads are processed.

Per-client isolation (boundaries that hold)

  • • Separate workspaces (tenancy): Data, configs, users, and guidance library are logically isolated by client.
  • • No cross-client learning by default: No shared fine-tuning/embeddings; no pooled hints.
  • • Transparent guidance: Suggestions include the exact lines/pages they came from.
  • • Optional collaboration (future, opt-in): Federated patterns (generic, non-sensitive), or air-gapped/on-prem as needed.
Why “client data only” matters:
Your fields, completions, and vendors create unique failure patterns. Teams trust suggestions grounded in their own history—and it’s easier to show that no third-party data influenced a recommendation.
